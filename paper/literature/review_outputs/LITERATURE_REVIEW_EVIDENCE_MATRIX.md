## Synthesis by Thesis Function

The corpus separates into four principal evidence roles. First, representation-oriented KG recommenders show why graph structure can improve recommendation while also showing that graph use alone does not guarantee an inspectable native path (`wang2018ripplenet`, `wang2021kgin`, `lu2023vrkg4rec`). Second, path-reasoning approaches demonstrate that actual graph walks can be part of recommendation generation (`xian2019pgpr`, `xian2020cafe`, `song2019ekar`, `wang2022multilevelReasoning`). Third, surveys and quality frameworks establish that explanation form and explanation quality are heterogeneous and multidimensional (`zhang2020explainableSurvey`, `balloccu2022xrecsys`, `balloccu2022xrecsysFramework`, `ge2025trustworthyRecSurvey`, `markchom2026graphExplainableSurvey`). Fourth, benchmarking literature shows why dataset, protocol, and metric consistency are necessary for fair claims (`shevchenko2024recsysBenchmarking` and the RL survey group).

No single corpus paper combines canonical dataset truth, heterogeneous model views, a shared native-path export schema, validation gates, strict ranking metrics, multiple explanation properties, controlled trade-off sweeps, and explicit boundary detection. That absence is a synthesis of the reviewed scope, not a claim that no related system exists outside this bounded corpus.
