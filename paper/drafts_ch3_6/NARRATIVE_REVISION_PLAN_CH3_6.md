# Narrative Revision Plan for Chapter 3–6

## 1. Rewrite Principles

1. Preserve every fact, value, evidence path, citation status, figure and table number, chapter boundary, and registered caveat.
2. Place the analytical question or claim before procedural detail and numerical evidence.
3. Use evidence to support a stated inference, then explain the inference's dissertation-level implication.
4. Move exhaustive values to the existing tables and figures while retaining decisive exact values in the prose.
5. Keep validation, strict accuracy, alpha-sweep, ablation, and Amazon-Book boundary evidence explicitly separate.
6. Distinguish direct experimental findings, repository-supported descriptions, plausible interpretations, and unsupported causal claims.
7. Preserve the distinction between native explanation evidence and post-hoc explanation concepts.
8. End each chapter with the unresolved question that motivates the next chapter.

## 3. Paragraph Rewrite Pattern

Use this pattern where possible:

Claim: Open with the analytical point that the paragraph must establish.

Evidence: Present only the evidence needed to support that point, preserving exact values and paths where they are part of the original claim.

Interpretation: State what the evidence permits the dissertation to infer, with causal strength matched to the experimental design.

Implication: Explain why the inference matters to the chapter question or the dissertation argument, then connect it to the next paragraph or section.

## 4. What Not to Change

- Do not change facts, experimental values, rankings, conclusions, or caveats.
- Do not alter any evidence path, figure number, table number, citation status, or chapter boundary.
- Do not combine strict accuracy, alpha-sweep endpoints, ablation evidence, or validation evidence.
- Do not convert plausible mechanism interpretations into causal findings.
- Do not present Amazon-Book as a complete six-model experiment.
- Do not introduce a new recommender contribution, new experiment, new citation, or new claim.
- Do not edit the original Chapter 3–6 draft files.
